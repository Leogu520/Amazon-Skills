# Keyword and Attribute Priority

Use keywords to improve accurate discovery, not to maximize the number of indexed phrases in Title.

## Evidence Sources

Prefer sources in this order:

1. Structured product facts and the current Product Type Definition
2. Search Query Performance at ASIN level
3. Advertising search term reports with orders, sales, conversion, and spend
4. Top Search Terms and Product Opportunity Explorer
5. Existing organic query and listing performance history
6. Competitor wording and autocomplete as discovery inputs only

Do not treat competitor claims as facts about the user's product.

## Priority Classes

### P0: Mandatory Identity and Anti-Misbuy

Examples: core product type, model, count, dimensions, capacity, interface, fitment, age/weight range, and variation identity.

Keep in Title when the buyer needs the information before clicking. P0 outranks search volume.

### P1: Primary Query

The most accurate high-intent query that names the product. Prefer the wording customers actually use when it matches the product type.

### P2: Primary Differentiator

One verified feature or material that changes comparison behavior, such as `65W GaN`, `Leakproof`, or `Stainless Steel`.

### P3: Secondary Discovery

Use cases, secondary materials, less important features, and relevant synonyms. Move to Item Highlights, bullets, description, or backend terms when constrained.

### P4: Remove

Promotions, subjective adjectives, irrelevant high-volume terms, duplicate synonyms, unsupported claims, and spelling/plural variants already handled by Amazon.

## Data Interpretation

- Search Query Performance can show query volume, impressions, clicks, cart adds, and purchases for brand-enrolled sellers.
- Sponsored Products search term reports include terms with at least one ad click. Use them to validate paid-query performance, not as a complete picture of organic demand.
- A high-impression, low-click term may be too broad or may reveal a weak first impression.
- A high-click, low-conversion term may indicate mismatch, missing qualification, price issues, or detail-page weakness.
- A lower-volume term with strong conversion can deserve Title space when it precisely identifies the ASIN.

## Selection Test

For every proposed Title term, ask:

1. Does it accurately describe this exact ASIN?
2. Does it help identify the product or prevent a wrong purchase?
3. Is there performance evidence for its query contribution?
4. Can the same meaning be expressed with fewer standard characters?
5. Is another listing field a better home for it?

Document the answer when removing a historically important keyword.
